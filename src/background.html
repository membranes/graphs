Background

At present, teams across the United Kingdom's governments manually identify or detect words/strings of interest within documents for custom redaction, knowledge graph, criminal investigations, etc., purposes. In the case of redaction, a few teams have access to standard solutions, i.e., software solutions focused on detecting and/or redacting common personal data words/strings. Alas, these solutions do not address the needs of teams that need to identify or detect bespoke words/strings.

A viable approach involves the creation of custom models via named entity recognition machine learning algorithms. These algorithms can be trained by domain, leading to compact, domain specific, word/string detection models. These algorithms are also known as token classification algorithms. This project focuses on a single token classification problem. Note. The underlying transfer learning approaches are applicable beyond token classification, i.e., applicable to abstractive or extractive text summarisation, image classification, object identification, etc.
